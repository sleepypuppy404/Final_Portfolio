Project: Personas

The Sharpe Refactory Ice Cream Machine





Context

In UIUX design, one of the most important aspects is actually understanding your user. Its important to consider the type of person likely to use your product, what they might be thinking and feeling, who they are, and what their goal is when utilizing it. In short, their persona. This project is essentially an exercise in this process. Taking a mundane, every day object, then attempting to analyze and build up how and why a person may use it to better understand its design and possible improvements. So, I thought, what could be more delicious, and easier to analyze, then ice cream.






Research

Interface Research

The first place to begin with this project was of course, research on the interface and its design. This involved essentially visiting the interface, and getting a proper look at what exactly a user sees. The Sharpe Refactory Ice Cream machine is a Vevor 2450, and in addition to looking up relevant details on the machine, I drew my own plane view of the console to get a better idea of what users actually see.
Sketch of ice cream machine (left) and Vevor 2450 (right)

User Observation

After researching the interface, the next step was to observe users attempting to utilize the interface. While eating lunch at the ratty, I noted students attempting to utilize the machine. Based on the students I saw trying to use it, I made the following observations about users attempting to try and use it.
  • About half of users would sometimes walk up to the interface, stare at it, and then either serve themselves or walk away
  • Once a user decided to serve themselves, they would pull on a lever (left for vanilla, middle for mix, right for chocolate)
  • As the interface has no visible indicator, if the lever pulled did not produce ice cream, some users would just walk away dissapointed
  • If their initial lever of choice fails, some users will resort to pulling the other two to check if those work
  • Some users, rather than pull the levers, will look at the buttons in the interface's top panel (usually when it is out of service)

User Interview

I decided to interview others I ran into near the Sharpe Refactory's dubiously working ice cream machine, hoping that one of them would be willing to give me an interview. I conducted 3 interviews, asking all subjects the following questions and receiving the following answers:
Q: Why did you decide to use the ice cream machine?
A: Users cited enjoyment of ice cream, easy calories, and a good source of sugar.
Q: What are some aspects you like about the ice cream machine?
A: Users appreciated ease of simplicity and control, and fitting their affordances.
Q: What are some issues you have with the ice cream machine's interface?
A: Users were frustrated by downtime and overly complex panel as major issues.
Q: When you use this machine, what do you expect?
A: Users expect the machine to properly dispense icecream.
Q: If you could improve one thing about the interface, what would you change?
A: Users requested the removal of irrelevant buttons and more flavors, as well as a larger interface
